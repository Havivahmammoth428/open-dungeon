Open Dungeon allows you to play text-based roleplay games using artificial intelligence. The app runs entirely on your local computer. It creates story text and images without sending your data to any servers. You do not need accounts or internet access to use these features.

## 💻 System Requirements

To run Open Dungeon smoothly, your computer needs specific hardware. These tools rely on your graphics card and memory to generate images and text in real time.

*   Operating System: Windows 10 or 11
*   Graphics Card: NVIDIA GPU with at least 8GB of VRAM is recommended for best performance.
*   System Memory: 16GB of RAM or higher.
*   Storage Space: At least 20GB of free disk space to store the AI models.

If your computer meets these requirements, the software will generate images and text quickly. Computers with less power might experience delays during image creation.

## 🚀 Setting Up the AI

The first time you open the app, it needs to download the AI models. These models provide the intelligence for your stories and image generation.

1. Open the application.
2. Select the "Settings" menu.
3. Choose "Model Manager."
4. Click "Download" next to the recommended settings.
5. Wait for the green checkmark to appear once the models download fully.

The software uses Ollama to manage text and FLUX for image creation. These tools operate in the background so you can focus on your story. The app performs these tasks once during the initial setup to ensure everything works correctly.

## 🕹️ Starting Your First Game

After you set up your models, you can start your adventure.

1. Click "New Game" from the main menu.
2. Select a scenario from the list.
3. Type a brief description of your character.
4. Press "Enter" to begin the story.

The AI will write a paragraph of text. You can respond by typing your character's actions or speech in the box at the bottom of the screen.

## 🖼️ Creating Images

Open Dungeon adds images to your story automatically. 

*   The app observes the context of your conversation.
*   It sends prompts to the image generator locally.
*   The system displays the resulting picture within the chat window.

You can trigger an image generation at any time by clicking the "Imagine" button located near the text box. This uses your local hardware to paint a picture based on the current scene. Keep in mind that generating images requires your computer's full focus for a few seconds.

## 🔧 Managing Your Data

Everything you create stays on your hard drive. 

*   Saved games live in the "Records" folder.
*   The app stores AI models in a separate data folder to keep your system files clean.
*   You can delete any save file from the main menu if you want more space.

The software does not collect usage data or send feedback to any company. Your stories contain personal content, and local storage keeps this data private.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**Does this require an internet connection?**
No. You only need the internet to download the app and initial models. After that, you can disconnect your computer and continue playing offline.

**Why does my computer fan get loud?**
The AI models usage puts a heavy load on your graphics card. This heat is normal. The fan spins faster to cool the internal parts while the software does the complex math required for generating text and images.

**Can I use other models?**
Yes. Use the "Model Manager" to add new text models if you prefer different writing styles. The app supports many formats used by Ollama.

**How do I save my journey?**
The app saves your progress after every response. You can close the app at any time without losing your place. When you return, the "Continue Game" option appears on the start screen.

**Is it safe to run this?**
Yes. The software runs in a standard folder and does not make changes to your operating system files. You can safely remove the program through your Windows settings whenever you want.

**What happens if the image generation fails?**
This usually means your computer runs out of memory. Try closing other programs like web browsers or video players to free up space. Restarting the app often fixes temporary memory issues.
